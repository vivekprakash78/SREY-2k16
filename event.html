Counter Strike : Global Offensive 

	1.General
1.1 The tournament head admins are Aditya Ray, Niravro Dutta, Srirup Bhattacharya and any other SREY official deemed a referee by sole discretion of above mentioned three people. They will have the last say on any subject. Decisions made by the admins supersede the rules and regulations in this document. 
1.2 These rules are written by SREYadmins. This board reserves the right to add, remove or modify the rules and regulations document at any time, without notice. We encourage everyone to read the rules and regulations from time to time to be sure you follow the latest version. 
1.3 By entering the tournament all players agree to be bound by and comply with the rules and regulations put forth by tournament officials. Players also agree that they fully understand and will comply with the fact that any tournament official decision supersedes the rules and regulations. 
1.4 SREY will provide computers and computer equipment i.e. (Mouse and Keyboard) that will be used during the tournament, you are only allowed to use your own equipment if you bring them. Teams will need to bring their own headsets and mouse pads during the tournament, the admins and the volunteers cannot help in this case. 
1.4.1You will have 15 minutes to set-up and start the match, if you are not ready within this time point rule (3.1.1) will take effect.
2.Tournament
2.1 Format – The size of the tournament will be dictated by how many players register for the tournament, with a maximum of 16 teams playing on Saturday. The eight best teams will advance to a playoff. Tournament play will be single elimination. 

2.2 Rounds Informations

2.2.1 1st Round& 2nd Round: A team will win the match when they wins 8 rounds, i.e. total number of rounds to be played is 16 at most. Team swap will occur after 8 rounds.

2.2.2 Quarter Finals:A team will win the match when they wins 11 rounds, i.e. total number of rounds to be played is 20 at most. Team swap will occur after 10 rounds.

2.2.3 Semi Finals & Finals:A team will win the match when they wins 16 rounds, i.e. total number of rounds to be played is 30 at most. Team swap will occur after 15 rounds.

2.2.4 Overtime: In case of a tie, the official rules of overtime will be followed in the tournament, 6 max rounds with starting money of 10000$.

2.4 Servers
2.4.1 SREY will provide servers for the tournament. 
2.4.2 All server configurations are the event administrator's responsibility.

2.5 Registrations: All teams are advised to register for the tournament before 12 PM of 19th March, 2016 to be eligible for participating in the tournament. Re-registrations are not allowed.


3.Rules and Regulations 

3.1 Match start – Players should be ready to play at least 10 minutes before scheduled match start. Matches will start exactly when scheduled. If a player is not ready within 10 minutes after the scheduled match start, the opposing team will win the match by walk-over. 
3.1.1 Where a player is not prepared to play, awarding of a walk-over will be at the sole discretion of the referees. 

3.2 Interruptions
3.2.1 If a match is interrupted by a server crash or network problems, the player remaining on the server must take a screen-shot of the score and the match will be resumed with the score present in the screen-shot. If both players are disconnected and neither were able to take a screen-shot, the tournament officials will decide if the match should be replayed. 

3.3 In game team- and nick-names
3.3.1 All players must use the team name or tag and player nick used when they registered for the tournament. This applies everywhere (in-game, IRC, IM, etc). 
3.4 Match complaint
3.4.1 If a player feels they have been the victim of a rule violation during a match, the player must report about the problem to any of the administrators available in the arena. 

3.5 Score reporting – After a match both teams must enter and confirm the match score, if a team does not report the score within 15 minutes of a match, that team will be deemed the loser of that match. After entering the match score, a teamcannot complain about the score.

3.6 Warnings
3.6.1 A player can receive a warning if he commits any of the following offenses: 
3.6.1.1 Refuses to follow a tournament official's instructions. 
3.6.1.2 Arrives late. 
3.6.1.3 Uses insulting/bad language and/or displays bad behaviour. 
3.6.1.4 Partakes in any unsportsmanlike conduct. 

3.6.2 A player may be disqualified for any of the following reasons: 
3.6.2.1 By receiving more than one warning. 
3.6.2.2 By behaving violently. 
3.6.2.3 By any use of unlawful or unfair play. 
3.6.2.4 By misleading a tournament official. 
3.6.2.5 By not being present at the time listed on the match schedule. 
3.6.2.6 By using bugs that change the intended game play. 
3.6.2.7 By using any scripts. 
3.6.2.8 By using any custom files (example: Models and Skins)

Robo Cubix 

It is a Manual Robotics event in which participants are needed to build manually controlled bot which is capable of fighting against another bot for the maximum number of points. The objective of this event is not to immobilize your opponent’s bot or push it out of the arena but you have to fight for your area and score some points by performing some tasks. This event is not like conventional Robo-War type. It is planned in such a way that all the teams have to play strategically. It may not happen that the strongest bot will win. You have to play a strategic match. There will be two teams playing against each other. The team who will score maximum points will be the winner.

GENERAL RULES
•	250 is the registration fees for each team. 

•	The maximum number of participants allowed per team is 4. 

•	Teams are required to bring their own power supply to drive the bot. Only one plug point of 220V, 50Hz supply will be provided to each team. DC-DC converter is not allowed.

•	 Power supply : Maximum voltage up to 18 volt and maximum current up to 5 Amp.

•	Dimensions of the bot : 25cm * 25cm

•	Weight of the bot should be less than 4kg.

•	The first round will be a timer round.

•	From the second round the match will be held against each other.

•	There will be some objects in the arena which will carry some points. The bots will move those objects in a specified area and defend them from being stolen by the opponent. 

•	No bot is allowed to pick those objects up or grip them or incorporate the objects within its body. 

•	There will be a specific time limit for each match. 

•	Use of Lego Kits or any readymade mechanisms are NOT allowed. 

•	Any kind of mechanism which may damage the arena e.g. Flame, Acid, Hammer etc are NOT permitted. Any serious damage to the arena may lead to disqualification. 

•	Participants are not permitted to touch the bot while the match is running. If any such situation occurs where the bot is required to be operated by hand then the coordinators will take necessary steps. 

•	Participants are requested to bring their own tools and spare parts if required. 

•	The decision of the coordinators will be final.

Robo-Golf 

GENERAL RULES
•	1st round will be a timer round where every team will be given a fixed time during which they will have to pot the different colored ball in the holes of same color.

•	Only one robot will be present in the arena of that particular group which will be playing in the arena at that time. 

•	2nd round will be a competitive round where two teams will be competing in the arena at one single time to pot the balls.

•	Remaining rules will be same except that the difficulty level may increase.

•	3rd round will also be same as 2nd except that the difficulty level will be increased further with added obstacles such as inclined planes, etc.

•	There may be further surprise rounds with added bonus and negative markings in each round which you will be informed about during the event.
